Cachopo

My favourite spanish dish

Cachopo is a beloved Asturian dish hailing from Spain. It's made by sandwiching ham and melted cheese between two large veal or pork fillets, coated in breadcrumbs and fried until golden and crispy. Served with fried potatoes and sauces, cachopo is a flavorful comfort food that embodies the heart of Asturian cuisine.

Variations of cachopo now include creative fillings like spinach or seafood, making it a culinary delight appreciated by locals and tourists. With its rich flavors and hearty portions, cachopo is a must-try Spanish specialty, perfect for those craving a satisfying and indulgent meal.

Ingridients

  • 2 large veal or pork fillets
  • Slices of ham
  • Slices of cheese (often a melting cheese like mozzarella or a local variety)
  • Breadcrumbs
  • Flour
  • Eggs
  • Salt and pepper
  • Olive oil
  1. Prepare the Ingredients:
    2. Gather your veal or pork fillets, ham slices, cheese slices, breadcrumbs, flour, eggs, salt, and pepper.
  2. Assemble the Filling:
    3. Place slices of ham and cheese between the two fillets to create a sandwich-like structure.
  3. Breading the Cachopo:
    4. Coat the cachopo with flour, dip it in beaten eggs, and then coat it evenly with breadcrumbs.
  4. Frying:
    5. Heat olive oil in a pan over medium heat. Once hot, carefully add the coated cachopo and cook until golden brown on both sides. This ensures the meat is cooked through and the cheese inside melts.
  5. Drain and Serve:
    6. Place the cooked cachopo on paper towels to remove excess oil. Slice it into portions and serve it hot. Accompany it with fried potatoes and sauces of your choice.
  6. Optional Fillings:
    7. If you're adding extra fillings like spinach, mushrooms, or seafood, place them between the ham and cheese layers before breading.
